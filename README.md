# pizza-sales-analysis
Sales performance analysis using SQL / Power BI

## Project Overview
This project analyzes pizza sales data from January 2025 to December 2025 to evaluate overall sales performance, customer ordering behavior, and product-level performance. The goal is to generate insights that can support data-driven decisions around inventory planning, product strategy, and sales optimization.

## Key Metrics
- Total Revenue: $817.86K
- Total Orders: 21,350
- Total Pizzas Sold: 49,574
- Average Order Value: 38.31
- Average Pizzas per Order: 2.32

## Business Questions
- How do sales and orders trend over time?
- Which days and months generate the highest order volume?
- Which pizza categories and sizes contribute most to revenue?
- What are the best- and worst-performing pizzas by revenue, quantity, and orders?

## Tools Used
- SQL (data extraction and aggregation)
- Power BI (dashboarding and visualization)

## Key Insights

### Sales Trends
- Friday records the highest number of orders, while Sunday has the lowest.
- July is the strongest month by total orders, whereas October records the lowest.
- Order volume peaks during evenings and weekends, indicating high demand periods.

### Product Performance
- The Classic category generates the highest total revenue among all pizza categories.
- Large-sized pizzas account for the largest share of total revenue (45.89%).
- A small number of pizza products contribute a significant portion of total sales.

### Best and Worst Sellers
- Thai Chicken Pizza is the top-selling product by total revenue.
- Classic Deluxe Pizza leads in total quantity sold and total orders.
- Brie Carre Pizza is the lowest-performing product across revenue, quantity sold, and total orders.

## Outcome
This analysis highlights key revenue drivers, peak demand periods, and underperforming products.
